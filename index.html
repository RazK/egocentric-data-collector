Technical Proposal

Egocentric Data Collector

Proposed Development Approach for Fiverr Physical AI

1

Background

Prepared for discussion with Fiverr Physical AI. This proposal recommends a phased execution strategy based on my experience leading the development of a wearable smart-glasses platform spanning hardware, embedded systems, AI and software (bio below), scoped against the provided Head Band Camera Technical Specification (v1.2).

2

Project Objective

Build a lightweight, head-worn platform that captures synchronized first-person video and IMU data for training robots on real-world household tasks.

My role is to lead the technical execution of the project, making the key architectural decisions, identifying opportunities to accelerate development through existing technologies, and building the components that create unique value.

3

Proposed Execution Plan

Phase 1 — Alpha Prototype

Duration: 4–6 weeks

Objective

First functional wearable data-collection device, using commercially available hardware wherever practical.

Scope

  • Camera + IMU selection; Buy vs. Build evaluation
  • Wearable mechanical prototype
  • Android app: record, store, upload
  • End-to-end data collection pipeline

Target spec

1080p30 · H.264 · ~150° FOV · IMU ≥250 Hz · head-mounted · suitable for extended real-world data collection

Phase 2 — Internal Pilot

Duration: 2–4 weeks

Objective

Validate the complete solution with a small group of internal users.

Scope

Build 4–8 Alpha devices, deploy internally at Fiverr's Israel offices, and iterate on reliability, data quality, and architecture from user feedback. I visit on-site periodically throughout for hands-on support.

Outcome

A validated wearable platform and a prioritized roadmap toward production.

Phase 3 — Production Platform

High-level planning phase

  • Production hardware
  • Manufacturing
  • iOS support
  • Calibration
  • Hardware synchronization
  • Fleet management
Scope, timeline, team, and commercial terms for this phase follow from Alpha and Pilot findings.

4

Guiding Principles

Prioritize time-to-learning

Use COTS components where practical

Validate comfort and reliability early

Keep the 1,000-unit production target in view
