Technical Proposal

Egocentric Data Collector

Proposed Development Approach for Fiverr Physical AI

1

Background

Prepared for Fiverr, the client commissioning this initiative. As Co-Founder & CTO of BlinkAid, I took a wearable medical device from research to a funded, patented product — raising $1.2M+ while directing its hardware, firmware, signal processing, ML, and mechanical design end-to-end. This proposal applies that same ownership model here, scoped against Fiverr's Head Band Camera Technical Specification (v1.2), and phases delivery rather than building the full production system from day one.

2

Project Objective

Build a lightweight, head-worn platform that captures synchronized first-person video and IMU data for training robots on real-world household tasks.

3

Proposed Execution Plan

Phase 1 — Alpha Prototype

Duration: 4–6 weeks

Objective

First functional wearable data-collection device, using commercially available hardware wherever practical.

Scope

  • Camera + IMU selection; buy-vs-build call
  • Wearable mechanical prototype
  • Android app: record, store, upload
  • End-to-end data collection pipeline

Target spec

1080p30 · H.264 · ~150° FOV · IMU ≥250 Hz · head-mounted · ready for extended field testing

Phase 2 — Internal Pilot

Duration: 2–4 weeks

Objective

Validate the complete solution with a small group of internal users.

Scope

Build 5 devices, deploy internally with technical support, and iterate on reliability, data quality, and architecture from user feedback.

Outcome

A validated wearable platform and a prioritized roadmap toward production.

Phase 3 — Production Platform

High-level planning phase

Prepare for an initial production run of ~1,000 units: production hardware architecture, custom electronics if required, iOS support, hardware-level camera/IMU sync, factory calibration, mechanical optimization, manufacturing, and fleet management.

Scope, timeline, team, and commercial terms for this phase follow from Alpha and Pilot findings.

4

Guiding Principles

Prioritize time-to-learning

Use COTS components where practical

Validate comfort and reliability early

Keep the 1,000-unit production target in view
