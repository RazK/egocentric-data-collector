Technical Proposal

Egocentric Data Collector

Proposed Development Approach for Fiverr Physical AI

1

Background

Prepared for Fiverr as the client commissioning this initiative. As Co-Founder & CTO of BlinkAid, I took a wearable medical device from university research to a funded, patented product — raising $1.2M+ and personally directing the hardware, embedded firmware, signal processing, machine learning, mechanical design, and clinical validation behind it. That is the same end-to-end ownership this project needs, and I recommend approaching it through phased validation rather than attempting to build the complete production system from day one.

This proposal is scoped against Fiverr's own hardware requirements, detailed in the Head Band Camera Technical Specification (v1.2).

2

Project Objective

Develop a lightweight wearable platform for collecting high-quality first-person video and IMU data for training robots on real-world household tasks.

3

Proposed Execution Plan

Phase 1 — Alpha Prototype

Duration: 4–6 weeks

Objective

Develop the first functional wearable data collection device using commercially available hardware wherever practical.

Scope

  • Camera and IMU selection
  • Buy vs. Build evaluation
  • Wearable mechanical prototype
  • Android application
  • Video and IMU recording
  • Local storage
  • End-of-session upload
  • End-to-end data collection pipeline

Target

  • One functional Alpha device
  • 1080p at 30 FPS
  • H.264 video
  • Approximately 150-degree field of view
  • IMU sampling at 250 Hz or higher
  • Head-mounted form factor
  • Suitable for extended real-world testing

Phase 2 — Internal Pilot

Duration: 2–4 weeks

Objective

Validate the complete solution with a small group of internal users.

Scope

  • Build 5 devices
  • Internal deployment
  • Technical support
  • Reliability improvements
  • User feedback
  • Data-quality evaluation
  • Architecture refinements

Outcome

A validated wearable platform and a prioritized roadmap toward production.

Phase 3 — Production Platform

High-level planning phase

Prepare the system for an initial production run of approximately 1,000 units.

Expected topics include production hardware architecture, custom electronics if required, iOS support, hardware-level camera and IMU synchronization, factory calibration, mechanical optimization, manufacturing and supplier coordination, production testing, fleet management and large-scale deployment.

The detailed scope, timeline, team structure and commercial terms for this phase will be defined based on the findings from the Alpha and internal pilot phases.

4

Guiding Principles

Prioritize time-to-learning

Use COTS components where practical

Validate comfort and reliability early

Keep the 1,000-unit production target in view

5

Next Step

The immediate next step is a technical alignment call to confirm priorities, success criteria, team structure and the expected level of ownership.
